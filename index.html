01 / About

Built for students who want to understand risk beyond the textbook.

The Financial Crime Society creates a space for students to explore how financial crime operates in real-world environments — from money laundering and fraud to governance failures, sanctions exposure, and regulatory expectations.

02 / What We Do

Learning, discussion, and applied exposure.

The society connects academic learning with practical understanding through structured activities, case discussions, and professional engagement.

Research

Financial Crime Intelligence

We analyse AML/CFT themes, fraud patterns, sanctions risk, governance failures, and regulatory developments in a structured way.

Practice

Case-Based Learning

Members work through realistic cases involving onboarding, UBO structures, transaction monitoring, red flags, and risk-based decision-making.

Network

Professional Exposure

We aim to connect students with professionals across compliance, governance, audit, risk advisory, fintech, and financial services.

03 / Featured

Featured initiative.

A dedicated space for our most important programme, event, simulation, or professional collaboration.

Financial Crime & Governance Forum

A proposed discussion series exploring the connection between financial crime risk, governance structures, compliance culture, and institutional accountability.

The objective is to help students understand that compliance is not isolated. It operates within wider governance systems, board oversight, risk culture, and ethical decision-making.

  • AML/CFT and governance discussions
  • Professional speaker sessions
  • Student-led research briefings
  • Case study workshops

AML Case Study Workshops

Financial Crime Briefings

Student Career Pathways
