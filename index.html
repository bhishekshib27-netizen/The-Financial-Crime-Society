01 / About

Built for students who want to understand risk beyond the textbook.

The Financial Crime Society creates a focused space for students to understand how financial crime operates in real environments — from money laundering and fraud to governance failures, sanctions exposure, and regulatory expectations.

02 / What We Do

Applied learning, professional exposure, and structured discussion.

The society connects academic learning with practical understanding through case-based sessions, research briefings, campus engagement, and professional conversations.

Research

Financial Crime Intelligence

We analyse AML/CFT themes, fraud patterns, sanctions risk, governance failures, regulatory expectations, and emerging financial crime typologies.

Practice

Case-Based Learning

Members work through realistic scenarios involving onboarding, UBO structures, transaction monitoring, red flags, escalation, and risk-based decision-making.

Network

Professional Exposure

We aim to connect students with professionals across compliance, governance, audit, risk advisory, fintech, law, and financial services.
